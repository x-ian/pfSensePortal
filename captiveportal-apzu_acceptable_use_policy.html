Internet Acceptable Use Policy

Abwenzi Pa Za Umoyo (APZU) provides Internet access to APZU employees and staff of the supported Ministries, either though office computers or laptops. Additionally private systems might be connected. However all of us share one Internet connection. The primary purpose of this connection is to enable communication, work, and research. To ensure the primary intention of the Internet Access everybody must adhere to the Acceptable Use Policy. With this the rules of accepted Internet usage are outlined. Exception can be made after contacting the APZU-IT team.

The following activities are allowed during work hours

  • Accessing the Internet for WORK RELATED purposes such as research and product inquiries.
  • Downloading, printing and copying of WORK RELATED items only.

The following activities may be allowed during non-working hours

  • Use of personal email and chat programs (Yahoo, Gmail, Facebook, etc).
  • General Internet browsing of appropriate sites (see below for inappropriate sites).

The following activities are expressly forbidden

  • All music, video, and application downloads.
  • Large email attachments and file uploads bigger than 3 Megabyte
  • Excessive video and music streaming from sites like youtube.com
  • Inappropriate Internet browsing and streaming (pornography, racially insensitive sites, etc).
  • Video chats and phone calls through computers.
  • Sending racially-, sexually- or general harassing emails.
  • Any attempts to bypass network security or to change network infrastructure.

When violations against this policy are detected, the APZU-IT team has the right to disconnect single computers without notice from the network if frequent violations are detected.

For further questions or clarifications please see the APZU-IT team at the help desk hours on Friday.
